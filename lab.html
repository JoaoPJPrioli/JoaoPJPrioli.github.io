Our Lab

The Smart Manufacturing & Disassembly Lab (SMDL) at North Carolina A&T State University is equipped with state-of-the-art machinery and robotic systems that support cutting-edge research in automation, smart manufacturing, and end-of-life product processing. Our facility provides the experimental infrastructure needed to develop and validate computational frameworks for CAD-based disassembly, human-robot collaboration, and data-driven manufacturing intelligence. The lab bridges the gap between theoretical research and real-world industrial applications, enabling our team to tackle pressing challenges in sustainable manufacturing and circular economy practices.

Robotic Workstation

A versatile robotic workstation used for developing and testing automated assembly and disassembly sequences. Supports research in human-robot collaboration and adaptive manipulation strategies for end-of-life product processing.

Smart Automated Assembly Line

A smart automated assembly line enabling research into intelligent production workflows, sensor integration, and data-driven manufacturing optimization.

CNC Machines 1

Computer numerical control machining equipment supporting precision manufacturing experiments and process parameter research for smart production systems.

CNC Machines 2

Additional CNC machining resources providing expanded capacity for multi-axis precision work and advanced manufacturing process studies.

Plastic Injection and Blow Molding

Equipment for plastic injection and blow molding processes supporting research into polymer manufacturing, material recovery, and end-of-life reprocessing.

Lab Overview

An overview of the SMDL facility showcasing the integrated research environment where smart manufacturing experiments are conducted. The lab space supports collaborative projects across robotics, automation, and sustainable manufacturing research areas.
