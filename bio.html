// about

Dr. Joao Prioli is an Assistant Professor in the Department of Industrial & Systems Engineering at North Carolina A&T State University, where he directs the Smart Manufacturing & Disassembly Lab (SMDL). He received his Ph.D. and M.S. in Industrial and Systems Engineering from Wayne State University and holds a B.S. in Electrical Engineering from the University of Uberlândia. His interdisciplinary background — bridging electrical engineering fundamentals with advanced industrial systems research — uniquely positions him to tackle complex challenges at the intersection of automation, sustainability, and intelligent manufacturing. Dr. Prioli's expertise drives the development of next-generation solutions that are critical to accelerating the transition toward a circular economy, where products are designed, used, and recovered with minimal waste and maximum resource efficiency.

Dr. Prioli's research focuses on advancing automation solutions to support the circular economy, with a particular emphasis on smart manufacturing, CAD-based disassembly, and human-robot collaboration. His work explores data-driven modeling and automated disassembly processes to enable sustainable production and resource recovery. By integrating computational intelligence with robotic systems, he is pioneering frameworks that allow machines to interpret product geometry, collaborate safely alongside human workers, and adaptively execute end-of-life disassembly — dramatically reducing the cost and environmental footprint of material recovery. This research has far-reaching implications for industries ranging from electronics recycling to automotive remanufacturing, contributing directly to global sustainability goals and the development of a more resilient, resource-smart manufacturing sector.
